About

It all began when all the Greek gods, except Eris (nymph goddess of discord and strife), were invited to the wedding of the glorious mortal Peleus and the goddess Thetis. Out of revenge and jealousy, Eris threw a golden apple to the crowd with an inscription reading “To the Fairest.” Aphrodite, Hera, and Athena were all in the same crowd, and they all looked down on the apple, claiming it belonged to them. Hence, they began deciding who was the most beautiful, though ultimately, they took this decision to Trojan Prince Paris. The three goddesses got undressed in front of Paris and asked him to choose who was the most beautiful, though because he couldn’t decide, the goddesses resorted to bribing him.

Where is it found?

The Apple of Discord has been thrown by Eris at Mount Pelion in Ancient Greece, and this triggered a war that occurred in Troy, which is situated in modern-day Turkey.

Species and Gender

Eris: Female Nymph Goddess

The goddess of strife and discord. Eris threw the golden apple at the wedding of Peleus and Thetis.

Hera: Female Goddess

The goddess of marriage. Hera was one of the three goddesses who claimed the apple. She is known for being a tall, striking woman with big full eyes.

Athena: Female Goddess

The goddess of wisdom and warfare. Athena was another of the three goddesses who wanted the apple. She was a majestic figure with full lips and gray eyes.

Aphrodite: Female Goddess

The goddess of love and beauty. Aphrodite was the third goddess who desired the apple, and she ultimately received it. She is the most beautiful woman ever seen, with long blonde hair, blue eyes, and feminine curves.

Paris: Male Prince (Mortal human)

A Trojan prince, Paris was asked to choose which of the three goddesses is the fairest to be given the apple. He is portrayed as a handsome and attractive man in painting representations.

Helen of Sparta: Female Mortal

Helen was a human woman, the wife of Menelaus, King of Sparta. She is the central figure in the Trojan War, whose abduction by Paris, a Trojan prince, is the catalyst for the conflict. She is the most beautiful mortal during the time.

The History

The Apple of Discord is an ancient Greek myth that followed the tale of the golden apple that was dropped by Eris, Greek nymph goddess of Discord and Strife. The apple was dropped during the wedding of Peleus (son of Aeacus and king of Aegina), and Thetis (a sea nymph and a goddess of the sea). Eris, who was offended for being the only uninvited god in the joyous event, vindictively dropped the apple among three powerful goddesses: Athena, Aphrodite, and Hera.

The apple was inscribed with the words “To the fairest,” which eventually led to a vanity-fueled argument among the three goddesses. Zeus, God of Thunder (who would usually handle petty disputes) did not want to risk offending any of the goddesses, so he left the responsibility to the Trojan prince, Paris, to provide the apple to the worthiest goddess. Each of the three goddesses offered Paris a unique gift to earn his favor.

Goddess Athena offered unmatched wisdom and skill in war. Alongside her, Hera offered power and domination over all of Asia. Lastly, Aphrodite offered the most beautiful woman in the world, who was none other than Helen of Sparta (who, at the time, was already married to King Menelaus of Sparta). Tempted by Aphrodite's offer, Paris ultimately accepted her offer and pursued Helen of Sparta, allowing Aphrodite to steal Helen and enchant her with love for him, which eventually started the Trojan war.

The war between the Trojans and the Achaeans lasted 10 years, all due to the dispute of who claimed Helen. Eventually, the war came to an end in the Achaeans' favor, and as a result, Helen was given back to Menelaus. Regardless, despite how much he loved Helen in the past, he was intending to kill her as soon as she was returned. However, as Helen was kneeling for Menelaus' mercy, he eventually softened up and offered to spare her life if she killed her two perfect children that she had with Paris. To which she hastily agrees.

Weapons

The Apple of Discord

Inscribed with the words “To the Fairest.” This act was a petty trick to stir up scandal upon the gods, caused by Eris’s spite towards the fact that she was the only uninvited god to the wedding. This small act eventually led to the Trojan War. Now, it is also a saying for when something small turns catastrophic, as in “sowing the seeds of discord.”

“To the Fairest”

Apple of Discord

Eris throws the apple, which is inscribed saying "To the Fairest," in an even distance between the three goddesses, provoking a dispute of vanity that Paris was forced to declare an end to.

"[Paris] had preferred the goddess Aphrodite, and now he was dead and his city ash."

(Circe 352).

This validates that Paris' decision was fatal. By providing the Apple of Discord to Aphrodite and hence pursuing Helen of Sparta, he determined his fate. Paris stole the woman that Menelaus loved dearly, and therefore he declared war, in which Paris was unable to be protected, since Aphrodite has the sole power of controlling love.
